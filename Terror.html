Terror Knows No Borders

The Pahalgam terror attack in Jammu and Kashmir was one of the deadliest incidents in the region since the Pulwama strike in 2019. The attack, carried out by The Resistance Front (TRF), a proxy of the Pakistan-based Lashkar-e-Taiba, targeted tourists visiting Baisaran meadows, a scenic destination near Pahalgam. Tragically, 26 people lost their lives, including a Nepali citizen, Sudip Neupane. Nepal has strongly condemned the attack, with Prime Minister KP Sharma Oli and Foreign Minister Arzu Rana Deuba expressing deep sorrow and solidarity with India. Nepal’s government reaffirmed its stance against terrorism, emphasizing the need for regional cooperation to combat such threats. The attack has also led to heightened security measures in Jammu and Kashmir, with Indian authorities launching a massive counter-terror operation. The incident has sparked international reactions, with leaders from various countries, including the UK and China, condemning the attack and expressing support for India. Nepal’s leadership has urged stronger regional collaboration to prevent future attacks and ensure the safety of its citizens abroad.
